## How to Choose a Land Surveyor in Sioux Falls

**Check their South Dakota PLS license first.** Every land surveyor working in South Dakota must hold a Professional Land Surveyor (PLS) license issued by the South Dakota Surveying Board. This isn't optional or a nice-to-have — it's the legal requirement. The board maintains a public roster; verify the name, license number, and whether there are any disciplinary actions. If they can't or won't give you their license number, stop there.

**Ask about their NSPS membership and whether they're bonded.** The National Society of Professional Surveyors (NSPS) membership signals ongoing education and professional standards. Bonding protects you if something goes wrong. Neither is a substitute for licensing, but together they're a good signal of someone who takes their work seriously.

**Get a written scope and fee estimate before work begins.** This is where most people get burned. A boundary survey, ALTA/NSPS title survey for a commercial transaction, and a topographic survey can look similar to a non-surveyor but cost vastly different amounts. A professional surveyor will break down exactly what you're getting — how many property corners they'll locate, what records they'll research, how long the turnaround is, and what deliverables you'll receive (PDF, hard copy, digital file format). If they quote over the phone without understanding your property, that's a red flag.

**Ask how long they've been doing residential vs. commercial work.** A surveyor with 15 years of commercial development experience may not be the fastest choice for a simple residential boundary survey — and vice versa. Neither is bad; it's just about fit. For Sioux Falls, a lot of work involves property lines in older downtown parcels, new suburban development in the northeast, and agricultural-to-residential transitions on the city's edges. Find someone who's done work in your area.

**Pro Tip:** In South Dakota, if you're involved in a mortgage transaction, your lender will often require an ALTA/NSPS survey — not just a boundary survey. Ask your title company or lender what they need *before* you hire. This saves money and avoids re-work.

## What to Expect

A simple residential boundary survey in Sioux Falls typically runs $500–$1,200, depending on property size and how many corners need locating. ALTA/NSPS surveys for commercial transactions or complex parcels range from $2,000–$5,000+. Turnaround is usually 1–2 weeks for standard work, longer if the surveyor needs to research old deeds or access difficult terrain.

**Reality Check:** If someone quotes you $400 for a full boundary survey with research and a detailed report, they're either operating on volume (which sometimes means corners cut) or they don't understand your property. Similarly, if the quote is $4,000 for a simple residential lot, ask why. The difference usually comes down to complexity — setback verification, easement research, topographic data, or boundary dispute history will all add time and cost.
