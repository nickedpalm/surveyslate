## How to Choose a Land Surveyor in Manchester

**Verify New Hampshire licensure first.** Every surveyor here should be a licensed Professional Land Surveyor (PLS) with a New Hampshire seal. Don't assume—call and ask to verify their license number with the state. This takes 90 seconds and eliminates 80% of the problems.

**Ask what type of survey you actually need.** A boundary survey for a residential purchase ($500–$1,500) is completely different from an ALTA/NSPS survey for commercial real estate ($3,000–$5,000+) or a topographic survey for development. Most surveyors will ask the right questions, but some will oversell. Know what your lender or attorney actually requires before you call.

**Check their turnaround time upfront.** Manchester's active real estate market means surveyors book out. If you're closing in 10 days, don't hire someone who quotes 3 weeks. Verbal promises aren't binding—get the timeline in writing.

**Ask if they've worked in your neighborhood.** Manchester spans from downtown to suburban developments, and deed records vary wildly depending on which area you're in. A surveyor who knows the Millyard District's tangled industrial-era boundaries is worth more than someone working off generic templates.

**Pro Tip:** Call the title company or attorney handling your transaction first. They already know which surveyors deliver on time and get it right the first time. This is the fastest way to a shortlist.

## What to Expect

A boundary survey typically takes 2–4 weeks from order to delivery, though rush jobs are possible if you're willing to pay a premium. You'll provide the deed, address, and details about what you need the survey for—the surveyor does the rest. They'll physically mark the property, research deed history and prior surveys, check municipal records, and deliver a signed, sealed document that's legally defensible.

Pricing depends entirely on complexity. A simple residential boundary survey in a newer subdivision might run $600–$1,200. If your property has a murky deed history, contested boundaries, or sits on difficult terrain, expect $2,000–$5,000+. ALTA surveys for commercial work are almost always in the $3,000–$5,000 range because they require title insurance company sign-off and more detailed research.

**Reality Check:** Don't cheap out on the survey. The $300 you save by hiring the lowest bidder often costs $3,000 in title delays or disputes later. A licensed surveyor's seal is their professional reputation—they're not taking shortcuts.
