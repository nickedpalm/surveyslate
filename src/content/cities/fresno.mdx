## How to Choose a Land Surveyor in Fresno

**Verify California licensure first.** Every surveyor in California must hold a Professional Land Surveyor (PLS) license issued by the California Department of Consumer Affairs. Check their name on the [CDCA license lookup](https://www.cdca.ca.gov/)—seriously, do this before anything else. An unlicensed person doing survey work is committing a crime and leaving you liable.

**Ask what type of survey you actually need.** This matters because it changes scope and cost. A simple residential boundary survey (identifying property lines) runs $500–$1,500. An ALTA/NSPS survey (the detailed, title-company standard for commercial transactions) is $2,000–$5,000+. A topographic survey or construction staking costs more. Most surveyors will ask you clarifying questions on a phone call; if they immediately quote a price without understanding your project, that's a red flag.

**Look for NSPS membership or CST credentials.** Membership in the National Society of Professional Surveyors or a Certified Survey Technician (CST) on staff signals the firm invests in staying current. It's not mandatory—California doesn't require it—but it matters.

**Get 2–3 quotes, but ignore rock-bottom prices.** If someone's quote is 40% below market, they're either cutting corners or they've misunderstood your job. Survey quality is invisible until a dispute lands in court; that's not where you want to find out you hired the cheapest option.

**Ask about turnaround time upfront.** A straightforward residential boundary survey typically takes 2–3 weeks. ALTA surveys and complex work take longer. If you're on a closing timeline, say that now.

> **Pro Tip:** In Fresno's agricultural and development-heavy market, boundary disputes over older properties are common. If your property predates modern title records, ask the surveyor upfront if they've worked with similar properties and what that typically costs. It might be more than a standard survey.

## What to Expect

Your surveyor will visit the property, locate existing monuments or establish new ones, measure distances and angles, and produce a sealed and signed plat (map) showing your property boundaries. The whole process—from site visit to final deliverable—typically runs 10–20 business days for standard work. You'll get a professional document that satisfies title companies, lenders, and courts. Every signature and seal on that document represents the surveyor's legal liability and their professional reputation.

Pricing breaks down roughly like this: residential boundary surveys start around $500–$800 for straightforward suburban lots; $1,500–$2,500 if the property is larger, rural, or has title record gaps; ALTA/NSPS surveys for commercial transactions, $2,500–$5,000+; specialty work (topographic, construction staking, elevation certificates for flood zones) varies widely based on acreage and complexity.

> **Reality Check:** The cheapest survey isn't always wrong—but it often reflects less experienced staff, slower turnaround, or incomplete research. Don't confuse "affordable" with "bargain." A $600 survey from a solo operator with 30 years of local knowledge may be smarter than a $400 job from a high-volume mill.
