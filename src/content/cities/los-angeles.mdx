## How to Choose a Land Surveyor in Los Angeles

**Verify California licensure first.** A PLS (Professional Land Surveyor) license in California means they've passed the NCEES exam, logged thousands of documented hours in the field, and meet the state's continuing education requirements. Check the California Department of Consumer Affairs — BPELSG (Board of Professional Engineers, Land Surveyors, and Geologists) maintains the public license registry. If they're not there, they're not licensed. Period.

**Match the survey type to your project.** A boundary survey for a residential property ($500–$1,500) looks nothing like an ALTA/NSPS survey for a commercial real estate transaction ($2,500–$5,000+) or a topographic survey for development work. Ask what surveys they specialize in. Someone's portfolio matters. If you're doing a commercial deal and they've mostly done residential boundary work, they may underestimate complexity.

**Ask about turnaround and insurance.** Los Angeles properties often involve complex chains of title, easements, and overlapping jurisdictions. A competent surveyor will give you a realistic timeline — usually 5–14 business days depending on county records availability and site conditions — and carry professional liability insurance. Don't hire someone who can't show you proof of coverage.

**Check if they're NSPS members.** The National Society of Professional Surveyors (NSPS) maintains professional standards. Membership isn't required, but it signals someone who's stayed current with industry best practices and ethical guidelines. It's a soft filter, but useful.

**Pro Tip:** Get three quotes, but don't hire the cheapest. If a survey that should cost $2,000 suddenly costs $800, they're either desperate, cutting corners, or misunderstood your project. Boundary disputes cost six figures in legal fees. Save $1,200 on the survey, lose $200,000 to litigation.

## What to Expect

A boundary survey in Los Angeles typically runs $500–$2,000 depending on property size, accessibility, and how old the existing records are. County records in Los Angeles are digitized but fragmented across multiple jurisdictions — the City of LA, County assessor, and often adjacent city departments — which adds time. ALTA/NSPS surveys (required for most commercial transactions) run $2,500–$5,000+ because they involve title search integration, insurance company requirements, and higher liability. Topographic and construction staking surveys vary wildly based on site complexity.

Most surveyors will need 5–10 business days minimum to pull records, schedule site work, and prepare the final plat. Rush jobs exist but cost more and increase error risk.

**Reality Check:** If someone quotes you a timeline of 48 hours for a boundary survey in Los Angeles, they haven't actually pulled the records yet. They're either overselling speed or planning to skip steps.
