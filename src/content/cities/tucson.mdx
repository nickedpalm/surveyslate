## How to Choose a Land Surveyor in Tucson

**Check the PLS License.** Arizona requires all surveyors to be licensed by the State Board of Architects, Engineers, and Land Surveyors. A legitimate firm will list their PLS number on their website or quote without you asking. If they don't, move on. The PLS is legally responsible for every survey they deliver — it's not a credential they're hiding.

**Get Specific About Survey Type.** Boundary survey, ALTA/NSPS title survey, topographic, elevation certificate, construction staking — they're not all the same job or price. Most residential boundary surveys run $600–$1,200 in the Tucson market. ALTA surveys (required for commercial transactions and refinancing) start at $1,500 and climb fast depending on property complexity. Know what you actually need before you call.

**Ask About Turnaround and Rush Fees.** Standard timeline in Tucson is 7–14 business days for a straightforward boundary survey. If you're closing in three days, expect a rush fee (usually 25–50% premium). A surveyor who quotes you five-day turnaround on a complex property is either underselling or overworked — both are bad signs.

**Verify Their Insurance.** Licensed surveyors carry errors and omissions insurance because mistakes happen (property line disputes, missed easements, setback violations). A one-line confirmation from them that they carry E&O is worth asking for. It's not negotiable.

**Pro Tip:** Call the Arizona Board of Architects, Engineers, and Land Surveyors (602-364-3150) to verify licensure and check for complaints on any firm you're considering. Takes five minutes, saves weeks of headaches.

## What to Expect

A typical Tucson survey job starts with a site visit, property deed research, and a walk of the boundaries. You'll get a sealed plat map showing property lines, dimensions, easements, and any encroachments — this is the deliverable you hand to your lender, attorney, or developer. Pricing scales with complexity: a half-acre residential lot in an established neighborhood might run $650–$900; a multi-parcel commercial site with utility conflicts and easement research could hit $3,500–$5,000+. Most firms require a deposit (30–50% of the quote) upfront.

Turnaround averages 10 business days for standard work. This isn't slow — it's careful. Your surveyor is researching public records, checking for adverse possession claims, and cross-referencing utility easements. Rush it, and you're asking them to skip steps.

**Reality Check:** If a quote seems too low (under $400 for a boundary survey), they're either desperate or planning to cut corners. If it's sky-high without clear explanation of scope creep, ask why. A transparent surveyor will itemize the work — research, site visit, plat drafting, certification.
