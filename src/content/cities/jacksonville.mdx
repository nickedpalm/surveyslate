## How to Choose a Land Surveyor in Jacksonville

**Verify Florida licensure first.** Every licensed surveyor in Florida carries a PLS (Professional Land Surveyor) credential and a license number you can cross-check with the Florida Department of Business and Professional Regulation. If they won't give you a license number, move on. There's no shortcut here.

**Match the survey type to your actual need.** A simple boundary survey ($500–$1,500) answers one question: where's the property line? ALTA/NSPS title surveys ($2,000–$5,000+) are what title companies and lenders require for commercial transactions — they're more thorough and cost more. Topographic surveys, construction staking, elevation certificates, and subdivision platting are all different animals with different price tags. Describe your project clearly, and a good surveyor will tell you exactly which type you need.

**Ask about turnaround and availability.** Jacksonville's real estate market moves fast. If a surveyor quotes three weeks and you need results in five business days, that's a conflict before you hire them. Local surveyors familiar with Jacksonville's permitting and title office processes will typically move faster than someone based two hours away.

**Check for multi-state credentials if applicable.** If your project touches state lines or you're working with a title company that covers multiple states, ask if they're licensed in Georgia or other neighboring states. A PLS from one state doesn't automatically transfer.

**Pro Tip:** Don't choose based on price alone. A $400 survey from someone who misses a boundary easement costs you $15,000 in legal fees later. Get two or three quotes, compare scope (not just price), and hire the one who listens carefully to your specific situation.

## What to Expect

A straightforward residential boundary survey typically takes 1–2 weeks from contract to final plat. Commercial ALTA work runs 2–4 weeks depending on complexity and title search delays. You'll sign a contract, provide access to the property, and receive a sealed survey document signed by a licensed PLS — that seal is what makes it legally binding for real estate transactions, title insurance, and boundary disputes.

Pricing ranges from $500 for a simple residential survey to $5,000+ for complex commercial or multi-parcel work. Factors that push costs up: property size, accessibility, title history complexity, whether they need to search old records, and how many corners require setting new markers.

**Reality Check:** If a quote seems suspiciously low (like, half the market rate), ask what's excluded. Some surveyors quote the site work only and hit you with research and title search fees later. Get the full scope in writing before you commit.
