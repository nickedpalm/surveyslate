## How to Choose a Land Surveyor in Little Rock

**Check their Arkansas PLS license first.** Every surveyor who signs a survey report in Arkansas must hold a Professional Land Surveyor (PLS) license issued by the Arkansas Board of Registration for Professional Engineers and Land Surveyors. Pull their name on the board's website — if they're not there, they're not licensed. This takes 90 seconds and saves you from hiring someone who can't legally deliver what you're paying for.

**Match the survey type to the job.** A $600 residential boundary survey is completely different from a $3,500 ALTA/NSPS title survey required for commercial real estate transactions. If you're refinancing a house, you need a basic boundary or mortgage survey. If you're buying commercial property or subdividing land, you need ALTA. Ask the surveyor upfront which type you need — if they don't clarify, that's a red flag.

**Ask about turnaround time and the real timeline.** Most surveyors quote 5-7 business days once they start fieldwork. What they don't always tell you: they might be booked out 2-3 weeks before they even schedule your property walk. If your closing is in 10 days, ask if they can prioritize or refer someone who can. Honesty here matters.

**Verify NSPS membership or CST credentials.** National Society of Professional Surveyors (NSPS) members and Certified Survey Technicians (CSTs) tend to stay current on Arkansas survey standards. It's not a deal-breaker if they're not members, but it's a signal they're invested in the profession beyond the minimum state requirement.

**Pro Tip:** If a surveyor is vague about pricing or won't give you an estimate over the phone, they're likely not organized enough to handle your deadline. Good surveyors price transparently — they know their own costs and can tell you in minutes whether you're looking at $500 or $5,000.

## What to Expect

You'll typically submit a survey request by phone or email with basic property info (address, current deed if you have it). The surveyor will schedule a fieldwork date, walk your property lines, and gather records from the Pulaski County Assessor's office and deed archives. The final report — sealed and signed by the PLS — takes another few days after fieldwork. Total time from request to deliverable is usually 2-4 weeks, depending on how booked they are and how clear your property records are.

Pricing depends on property size, complexity, and survey type. Residential boundary surveys run $500-$1,500. ALTA surveys for commercial work or title work typically run $2,000-$5,000+. Topographic surveys and construction staking cost more. Don't lowball yourself — a cheap survey that misses a boundary error is worthless and potentially expensive if a dispute surfaces later.

**Reality Check:** The surveyor's fee is often one of the smallest costs in a real estate transaction, but it's one of the easiest to cut. Resist that temptation. A licensed, thorough surveyor in Little Rock is worth every penny.
