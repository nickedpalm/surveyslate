## How to Choose a Land Surveyor in Jackson

**Check licensure first.** Mississippi issues Professional Land Surveyor (PLS) licenses, and that stamp on the final plat is legally binding—it's not decoration. Verify it on the Mississippi Board of Licensure for Professional Engineers and Surveyors. If they're also NSPS (National Society of Professional Surveyors) members, that's a bonus; it usually means they keep up with standards and continuing education.

**Match the survey type to your need.** A residential boundary survey ($500–$1,500 for a standard lot) is straightforward work. An ALTA/NSPS title survey (required for most commercial real estate transactions or loans) runs $2,000–$5,000+ because it's detailed and demands liability insurance that backs it. Topographic surveys, construction staking, and elevation certificates all have their own scope and price. Asking a surveyor "Is this job a boundary or an ALTA?" tells you instantly whether they're listening or just nodding.

**Get turnaround in writing.** Jackson's real estate market moves at its own pace, but a simple boundary survey shouldn't take eight weeks. Standard residential work: 1–2 weeks. Commercial or complex litigation-related surveys: 2–4 weeks. If someone can't give you a ballpark timeline in the first phone call, move on.

**Ask about local knowledge.** Jackson's deed records, subdivision plats, and municipal records have quirks—old easements, irregular lot splits, monument hunting in areas where original corner markers are gone. A surveyor who's spent years in the Jackson market knows where those corners usually are and which county records are actually reliable.

> **Pro Tip:** If you're hiring a surveyor for a legal dispute or a major transaction, ask if they've worked with your attorney or title company before. Repeat relationships mean faster turnarounds and fewer back-and-forths over interpretation.

## What to Expect

A typical residential boundary survey runs $500–$1,500 and takes 1–2 weeks from kick-off to final plat. You'll provide the deed and legal description; the surveyor schedules a site visit (expect 1–3 hours depending on lot size and complexity), shoots property corners, checks against recorded plats, and then produces a sealed, signed plat showing bearings, distances, and your property's footprint relative to neighbors.

Commercial or title work is more complex. An ALTA/NSPS survey adds title research, utility location coordination, and higher precision standards—cost ranges $2,000–$5,000+ and turnaround is usually 2–4 weeks. You're paying for the surveyor's liability insurance, expertise in title standards, and the fact that this document will be scrutinized by attorneys and lenders.

> **Reality Check:** Pricing varies wildly because every lot is different. An easy corner-to-corner survey on a small residential lot costs far less than a 20-acre raw parcel with no visible corners or a multi-phase development requiring staking. Get a quote in writing before the site visit; legitimate surveyors will give you a ballpark estimate once they understand the scope.
