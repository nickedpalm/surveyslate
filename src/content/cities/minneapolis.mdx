# Land Surveyors in [Minneapolis](/locations/minnesota/minneapolis/), Minnesota

Finding a qualified land surveyor in Minneapolis shouldn't feel like detective work. You need someone licensed in Minnesota, fast, who won't vanish after delivering a PDF — and you need to know what you're actually paying for before someone shows up at your property with equipment. This directory cuts through the noise and connects you with vetted professionals who understand Minnesota property law, the specific quirks of the Twin Cities real estate market, and why your boundary dispute matters enough to get it right the first time.

## How to Choose a Land Surveyor in Minneapolis

**Check the Minnesota license first.** Every land surveyor in Minnesota must hold a Professional Land Surveyor (PLS) license issued by the state. Verify it on the [Minnesota Department of Labor and Industry website](https://www.dli.mn.gov/) — don't assume because someone has a website that they're actually licensed. This is not negotiable.

**Ask for ALTA/NSPS experience if you're buying property.** Most residential real estate transactions in Minneapolis require an ALTA/NSPS title survey. A surveyor who specializes in boundary work but hasn't done title surveys regularly will take longer and charge more. If they're doing your survey for a purchase, they should know the local title company expectations and can flag issues before closing.

**Verify experience with Minneapolis zoning and wetland issues.** Minneapolis has wetland regulations that affect surveys on many residential and commercial lots, especially near the Mississippi River corridor and around lakes. A surveyor who knows the city's zoning overlay districts and wetland setback rules will catch problems a surveyor from Greater Minnesota might miss.

**Call and ask about turnaround time upfront.** A simple boundary survey in town can take 5-10 business days if the surveyor isn't backlogged. Complex ALTA work or subdivision platting can take 3-4 weeks. Don't ask for an estimate without first asking when they're actually available to do the work — price doesn't matter if you close on your house before the survey is done.

**Pro Tip:** Get at least two quotes, but don't pick based on price alone. A surveyor quoting $300 cheaper might be underestimating the complexity of your property — or they might be hungrier for work and willing to cut corners. Ask what's included in the fee. Does it cover a site visit, digital deliverables, a bound report, or just a PDF?

## What to Expect

Most residential boundary surveys in Minneapolis run $500–$1,500, depending on lot size and whether the property is in an urban area (easier to locate monuments) or a newer development (sometimes vague). Title surveys for real estate transactions typically cost $800–$2,500. Complex commercial work, ALTA surveys with extensive research, or subdivision platting can easily exceed $5,000.

The process is straightforward: you contact the surveyor, describe the job, they give you a quote and timeline, they visit your property (usually taking 1–4 hours depending on size and complexity), they process the field data, and they deliver a signed and sealed document. Turnaround is typically 2–4 weeks for standard work.

**Reality Check:** If a surveyor quotes you $250 for a boundary survey in Minneapolis, they're either desperate or planning to cut corners. The going rate reflects field time, equipment, liability insurance, and Minnesota licensure — all non-negotiable costs. If it sounds too cheap, ask why.

## Local Market Overview

Minneapolis's real estate market moves fast, especially for residential properties in neighborhoods like Loring Park, Powderhorn, and the Northeast. You need a surveyor who can turn work around without sacrificing accuracy — tight closing timelines are the norm, not the exception. Additionally, many Minneapolis properties sit on older platted lots with unclear monument locations, which means more research time and potentially higher costs. A surveyor familiar with Hennepin County records and Minneapolis's specific platting history will save you money and headaches.
