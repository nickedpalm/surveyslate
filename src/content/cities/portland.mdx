# Land Surveyors in [Portland](/locations/oregon/portland/), Oregon

Hiring a land surveyor in Portland feels like it should be simple—you need property lines confirmed, a title company needs documentation, or you're about to drop serious money on real estate and want to know exactly what you're buying. But finding someone who actually knows Oregon's quirks, has current credentials, and won't ghost you halfway through is harder than it should be. This directory exists to cut through that.

## How to Choose a Land Surveyor in Portland

**Verify Oregon licensure first.** Not all states recognize out-of-state credentials the same way, and Oregon is particular about who can sign survey documents. Look for "PLS" (Professional Land Surveyor) on their materials—it's the legal requirement, not a nice-to-have. Many qualified surveyors also carry NSPS (National Society of Professional Surveyors) membership and CST (Certified Survey Technician) certifications on staff.

**Match the survey type to your actual need.** Boundary surveys ($500–1,500 for straightforward residential work) are the bread-and-butter. ALTA/NSPS surveys ($2,000–5,000+) are required for commercial transactions and title insurance, especially in Portland's competitive real estate market. Topographic surveys, construction staking, and elevation certificates all have different pricing and turnaround. Don't pay for complexity you don't need.

**Ask about local project history.** Portland's older neighborhoods—especially close-in SE and NE—have tangled deed histories and quirky lot configurations. A surveyor who's worked those areas knows the county records, understands common title issues, and can flag problems before they become expensive surprises.

**Get a written scope and timeline upfront.** This protects you both. The surveyor should tell you exactly what they're measuring, how long it takes (usually 2–4 weeks for residential, longer for commercial), and what it costs. If they're vague, move on.

**Pro Tip:** Call your title company or attorney first—they often have preferred vendors and can recommend someone they've worked with. That referral usually means faster turnaround and fewer miscommunications.

## What to Expect

Most Portland surveys start at around $500 for simple residential boundary work on a standard lot. Complex properties, commercial sites, or ALTA/NSPS work climb into the $2,000–5,000 range and beyond. The surveyor will visit your property, measure against recorded deeds and plat maps, set stakes or flags to mark boundaries, and deliver a sealed survey document (legally required in Oregon). Turnaround is typically 2–4 weeks unless you're in a rush—expedited work costs more.

**Reality Check:** Don't confuse a survey with a title search. You need both for most real estate transactions. The surveyor confirms the physical boundaries; the title company confirms who legally owns what. They're different deliverables from different professionals.

## Local Market Overview

Portland's real estate market moves fast, and title companies here expect surveys before closing—sometimes within tight windows. The city's mix of older residential neighborhoods, emerging commercial corridors, and high-value properties means surveyors regularly handle everything from tiny close-in Craftsman bungalows to complex multifamily development. Know what you need, get it in writing, and you'll avoid last-minute panic.
