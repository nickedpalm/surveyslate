## How to Choose a Land Surveyor in Boise

**Verify Idaho licensure first.** A PLS license is not negotiable. Idaho's licensing board maintains a searchable roster; check it before you talk pricing. Some surveyors hold licenses in multiple states and specialize in cross-border work, which matters if your property touches the state line. This isn't snobbery—it's the law. Their seal and signature on a survey mean they're legally responsible for the accuracy.

**Match the surveyor to your project type.** A boundary survey for a residential lot ($500–$1,500) looks different from an ALTA/NSPS title survey for commercial real estate ($2,000–$5,000+) or a topographic survey for a subdivision ($3,000+). Ask a potential hire what they've done in the last 12 months. If you need a mortgage survey and they specialize in construction staking, they can do it, but you're not getting their best work. Boise's boom has created demand for mixed-use and infill development—surveyors who've handled that tend to move faster.

**Ask about turnaround and local know-how.** Boise's Ada County assessor records are digitizing but still partially paper-based. A surveyor who knows which records are where saves you 1–2 weeks. Ask flat-out: "How long for fieldwork, and how long to deliver the final product?" If they dodge the question, keep calling. The best surveyors in Boise have waiting lists but will tell you upfront if they're backed up.

**Pro Tip:** If you're buying through a title company, ask them for a referral. They work with the same surveyors repeatedly and know who delivers clean work on time. Title companies won't recommend someone flaky—their business depends on it.

## What to Expect

A typical boundary survey takes 1–3 weeks from call to signed deliverable, depending on record complexity and scheduling. Fieldwork itself might be 1–2 days; the rest is research, calculation, and drafting. Price is usually per-survey, not hourly, so get a written quote before work begins. Don't negotiate down to the point of insanity—the cheapest quote often means cutting corners on research or spending less time in the field.

> **Reality Check:** Surveyors in Boise aren't getting cheaper. Land's worth more, liability insurance is higher, and the pool of licensed PLS professionals is tight. If a quote seems half the market rate, ask why. Sometimes it's because they're new and hungry. Sometimes it's because they're cutting corners.
