## How to Choose a Land Surveyor in Memphis

**Verify Tennessee PLS licensure first.** Every surveyor who signs and seals your deliverables must hold a current Professional Land Surveyor (PLS) license from the Tennessee Board of Licensing for Professional Engineers and Land Surveyors. Check the board's roster before you call. Unlicensed technicians can do fieldwork, but they can't legally sign your survey—and title companies won't accept it.

**Ask what kind of survey you actually need.** This matters. A boundary survey for a residential mortgage ($500–$1,200) looks different from an ALTA/NSPS title survey for a commercial transaction ($2,000–$5,000+). A topographic survey for a development site or elevation certificate for flood insurance adds its own scope and timeline. The right surveyor will ask clarifying questions about your end goal before quoting—if they just throw out a price, they're not thinking clearly about your project.

**Check turnaround time against your deadline.** Simple residential boundary surveys in Memphis typically run 5–10 business days; ALTA work can stretch 2–3 weeks depending on title commitment complexity and how long it takes to resolve encumbrances. If you're 48 hours from closing, a surveyor needs to tell you that's tight (not impossible, but tight). A firm that guarantees turnaround without understanding your property's history is cutting corners.

**Verify they have experience with your property type and location.** A surveyor solid on suburban Memphis residential boundary work might not have done much riverfront property or commercial subdivision platting. Ask for a reference or past project in your neighborhood or property class—especially if you're in Germantown, Collierville, or a trickier area where old deed descriptions get messy.

> **Pro Tip:** Call 2–3 firms and ask the same specific questions. You'll hear confidence in the ones who know Memphis's market quirks (deed inconsistencies in older neighborhoods, common encroachment issues, typical title insurance holdouts) versus generic answers.

## What to Expect

A standard boundary survey includes fieldwork (your surveyor and/or a CST—Certified Survey Technician—will stake the corners and measure), research (title search, deed review, plat analysis), and a signed, sealed deliverable you can hand to your lender, title company, or attorney. Pricing runs $500–$1,500 for straightforward residential; commercial or ALTA work with title insurance requirements typically $2,000–$5,000+. Memphis's market is competitive enough that you shouldn't get low-balled, but you also shouldn't assume the highest quote is the best.

Turnaround is usually 1–3 weeks for routine work, longer if there are boundary disputes or title complications. A surveyor should give you a written estimate and timeline upfront.

> **Reality Check:** Don't confuse a cheap survey with a good deal. A $300 boundary survey usually means the firm is either cutting quality corners or sandbagging the scope. The $500–$1,200 range for residential boundary work reflects actual professional labor, insurance, and equipment costs. If a quote seems too low, ask why.
