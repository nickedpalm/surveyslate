# Land Surveyors in [Milwaukee](/locations/wisconsin/milwaukee/)

Finding a qualified land surveyor in Milwaukee shouldn't feel like you're hiring based on Yelp reviews and hope. You're about to hand someone the authority to legally define your property lines, testify in court if things get messy, or unlock a $2M development deal — and getting the wrong person costs thousands in delays or disputes. This directory connects you with licensed PLS (Professional Land Surveyor) professionals in Wisconsin who know Milwaukee's quirky property records, work with local title companies, and actually answer their phones.

## How to Choose a Land Surveyor in Milwaukee

**Look for Wisconsin PLS licensure first.** Every surveyor on this directory holds a Professional Land Surveyor license issued by the Wisconsin Department of Safety and Professional Services. This isn't optional — it's the legal requirement. If someone quotes you without a license, you have no recourse if the work is wrong. Period.

**Match the survey type to your need.** A $500 residential boundary survey (common for homeowners resolving fence disputes) is completely different from a $3,000–$5,000+ ALTA/NSPS title survey that lenders require for commercial transactions. Know which one you actually need before you call. If you're selling or financing, ask your title company or lender — they'll specify.

**Ask about local experience.** Milwaukee's property records date back to the 1800s, and older plats have quirks — missing monuments, conflicting descriptions, subdivisions that predate modern standards. A surveyor who's worked downtown and near the lakefront knows how to navigate that mess. Someone fresh from the suburbs might not.

**Verify they carry errors & omissions insurance.** If a survey error costs you money (a boundary dispute, a construction halt), you want recourse. Licensed surveyors carry E&O insurance; it's standard. Ask for proof.

**Pro Tip:** Don't choose based on price alone. A survey that's $300 cheaper because they're cutting corners on fieldwork creates liability you'll discover when you're under contract. The difference between a $800 and $1,200 boundary survey often reflects time in the field, not greed.

## What to Expect

Turnaround is typically 5–10 business days for standard residential surveys, longer for complex ALTA work or if the property records are messy (Milwaukee has plenty of both). You'll pay upfront or at signing — most surveyors require a deposit to schedule. The final deliverable is a sealed, signed survey plan bearing the surveyor's professional stamp; only a licensed PLS can legally produce this.

Pricing depends on property size, access, record complexity, and whether you need just a boundary survey or something more involved (elevation certificates, topographic surveys, construction staking). A simple residential boundary in a suburban Milwaukee neighborhood might run $600–$900. A commercial ALTA survey or a property with title issues can easily hit $2,500–$5,000+.

**Reality Check:** If someone quotes you $300 for a boundary survey in Milwaukee, something's wrong. They're either cutting fieldwork, not licensed, or they'll hit you with surprise add-ons. Legitimate work costs what it costs.
