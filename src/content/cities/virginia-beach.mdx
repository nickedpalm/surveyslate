# Land Surveyors in [Virginia Beach](/locations/virginia/virginia-beach/), Virginia

You need a property line verified, a title survey pulled together for closing, or expert testimony about a boundary dispute—and you need someone who actually knows Virginia law, not a contractor who dabbles in surveying on the side. Finding a qualified, licensed land surveyor in Virginia Beach sounds straightforward until you start calling around and realize half the people answering phones aren't even Professional Land Surveyors. The other half are booked three months out. This directory cuts through that noise.

## How to Choose a Land Surveyor in Virginia Beach

**Check for Virginia PLS licensure first.** Every land surveyor in Virginia must hold a Professional Land Surveyor (PLS) license issued by the Virginia Board for Architects and Engineers. If they don't have it, they can't sign and seal a survey. Period. This isn't a credential you negotiate—it's the law.

**Ask what type of survey you actually need.** A simple residential boundary survey runs $500–$1,500. An ALTA/NSPS title survey (required for commercial transactions and title insurance) costs $1,500–$5,000+. A topographic or construction staking survey depends on acreage and complexity. The surveyor should be able to scope your project in a five-minute phone call and give you a ballpark. If they can't, move on.

**Verify they've worked in your specific context.** A surveyor experienced with residential waterfront properties in the Hampton Roads area understands tide datums, submerged land claims, and coastal easements. A commercial developer's surveyor knows subdivision platting and staking protocols. Ask for a recent example of similar work.

**Check NSPS membership and certifications.** NSPS (National Society of Professional Surveyors) membership signals ongoing professional development. CST (Certified Survey Technician) or LS (Licensed Surveyor) credentials on the team matter for complex work.

> **Pro Tip:** Turnaround time varies wildly based on title research delays and weather. A surveyor who quotes "three weeks" without knowing your property's history is guessing. Ask about their actual timeline once they've reviewed your deed and local records.

## What to Expect

A typical boundary survey involves deed research, field measurements, and a final plat—plan on 2–4 weeks depending on title complexity and weather. You'll pay upfront or at signing; most surveyors don't invoice after delivery. For ALTA/NSPS surveys, title work can add another week. Costs range from $500 for a straightforward residential boundary to $5,000+ for multi-parcel commercial work with easements and encroachments.

Turnaround pressure is real in Virginia Beach—the Hampton Roads region moves fast, and title companies have deadlines. A good surveyor builds buffer time into their quotes.

> **Reality Check:** The cheapest quote is almost never the best one. A $300 survey from someone unlicensed or cutting corners can cost you tens of thousands in a title dispute later. Virginia's licensing standards exist for a reason.
